Cultural Heritage Documentation

2021 - on going · Politecnico di Milano

The digital documentation of cultural heritage represents one of the biggest challenges for researchers in recent years. In constantly evolving natural as well as urban environment affected by human intervention and climate change, preserving the memory and the importance of CH landmarks asks for innovative and flexible approaches. Geomatics with photogrammetry, laser scanning, GPS and traditional topographic measurements - plays a crucial role in the process, enabling the accurate 3D reconstruction of complex built architectures, generating georeferenced 2D and 3D products that could support the planning of mantainance interventions as well as the dissemination of CH sites history. In order to provide access to such digital documentation to a wider audience of both experts and normal citizens, web-based exploration solutions are currently studied. In this framework, WebGL help building highly customisable interactive platforms for easy exploration and evaluation of sites.

Having these goals and concepts in mind, the following projects tried to delve in the subject:

Castello Farnese in Piacenza (Italy)

May 2021 - December 2023
Tools: PotreeJS, CesiumJS, HTML, CSS, JS

  • Multi-source survey (UAV-Photogrammetry, Terrestrial Laser Scanning), digital documentation of the current state of the site as well as historical models reconstruction.
  • Testing of 2 open-source WebGL for the exploration of 3D products: Potree.JS and Cesium.JS.
  • Implementation of a Potree-based platform for the spatio-temporal exploration of the site and storytelling of its history.
  • Working demo

Galleria Ricci Oddi in Piacenza (Italy)

May 2023 - January 2024
Tools: PotreeJS, HTML, CSS, JS, PHP, PostgreSQL

  • Multi-source survey (UAV-Photogrammetry, Terrestrial Laser Scanning, SLAM) and digital documentation of an art exhibition built complex in Piacenza (Italy).
  • Definition of an interactive web 3D platform with user-friendly shortcut for exploring the outdoor and indoor environment.
  • Integration of Potree-based viewer with database for retrieving artworks information.
